## Vector DB Use Case

Traditional keyword-based search fails in large legal documents because it relies on exact word matching. Legal documents often use complex language, synonyms, and varied phrasing, making it difficult for keyword search to capture the true meaning of the query.

For example, a clause related to "termination" might not explicitly use that exact word but instead use phrases like "end of agreement" or "contract conclusion." Keyword search may miss such results.

Vector databases solve this problem by using embeddings, which capture the semantic meaning of text. Instead of matching exact words, they compare the meaning of the query with the meaning of documents.

This allows semantic search, where similar concepts are matched even if the wording is different. As a result, legal professionals can find relevant information quickly and accurately, even in large 500-page contracts.
